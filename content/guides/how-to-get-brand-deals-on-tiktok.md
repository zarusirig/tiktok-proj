## How Brand Deals Work on TikTok

### What is a Brand Deal?

A brand deal (also called sponsorship or paid partnership) is when a company pays you to create content featuring their product or service.

- Spark Ads authorization (brand boosts your video)

## Negotiating Brand Deals

### What to Negotiate

1. **Rate** (obviously)
2. **Deliverables** (how many videos, what format)
3. **Timeline** (when content is due)
4. **Usage rights** (can brand repurpose your content? For how long?)
5. **Exclusivity** (can you work with competitors?)
6. **Creative freedom** (how much control do you have over content?)
7. **Payment terms** (50% upfront, 50% upon delivery recommended)

### Negotiation Tips

**Start higher than your minimum:**
If you'll accept $500, quote $650â€“$750. Brands often negotiate down 15â€“20%.

**Don't be the first to name a price:**
Ask: "What's the budget for this campaign?" Let them anchor.

**Bundle for higher total value:**
Instead of $500 for one video, offer $1,200 for three videos (saves them per-video cost, increases your total).

**Anchor on value, not follower count:**
"My audience has a 9% engagement rateâ€”3Ã— the industry averageâ€”which drives significantly higher ROI for brands."

**Use competing offers as leverage:**
"I have another offer for a similar partnership at $X. I'd prefer to work with youâ€”can you match?"

**Be willing to walk away:**
If rate is too low and they won't budge, politely decline. Underpricing hurts you and the creator economy.

## Red Flags in Brand Deals

### ðŸš© Avoid These Situations

**1. "We'll pay you in exposure/free product only"**
- **When it's okay:** If you're under 5K followers and product value is high
- **When to decline:** If you're 10K+ followersâ€”your content has monetary value

**2. "We need content posted immediately" (without fair rush fee)**
- Brands rushing often have poor planning; expect issues

**3. "You can't see the contract until you agree"**
- Always review contract before committing

**4. "We own all rights to your content forever"**
- Limit usage rights to 6â€“12 months

**5. "We'll pay you after the post performs well"**
- Payment should be for content creation, not results (you can't control algorithm)

**6. "Remove any negative comments about the product"**
- FTC requires transparency; you can't hide genuine customer feedback

**7. No written contract**
- Always get agreement in writing (email counts, but formal contract is better)

**8. Payment only after video hits X views**
- Your payment shouldn't depend on TikTok's algorithm

## Legal Requirements and FTC Compliance

### You MUST Disclose Paid Partnerships

**Legal requirement (FTC in US, similar rules globally):**
- Clearly disclose when content is sponsored
- Disclosure must be easy to see (not hidden)

**How to disclose:**
1. **Use TikTok's "Paid Partnership" label** (best practice)
   - Add when posting video
   - Visible to viewers

2. **Include #ad or #sponsored in caption**
   - At the beginning, not buried at the end

3. **Say it verbally in the video**
   - "Thanks to [Brand] for sponsoring this video"

**Bad disclosure:**
> #partner (too vague)
> #spon (not clear enough)
> Hashtag buried after 20 other hashtags

**Good disclosure:**
> "Paid partnership with [Brand]" label + #ad in caption + verbal mention

**Consequences of not disclosing:**
- FTC fines ($10,000â€“$43,000 per violation)
- Account suspension
- Loss of brand trust
- Legal liability

### How do I respond if a brand offers too little?

### Can I negotiate after signing a contract?
Generally no. Negotiate before signing. Read carefully before committing.

### Should I work with brands I don't use?

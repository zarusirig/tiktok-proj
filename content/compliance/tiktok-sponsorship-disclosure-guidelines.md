# TikTok Sponsorship Disclosure Guidelines: FTC Compliance for Creators (2025)

## Introduction

Properly disclosing paid partnerships on TikTok isn't just best practice—it's a legal requirement. Failure to disclose sponsored content can result in FTC fines up to $43,280 per violation, account suspension, and loss of brand trust. This comprehensive guide explains exactly how to comply with FTC disclosure requirements and TikTok's branded content policies.

**What you'll learn:**
- Legal requirements for sponsorship disclosure (FTC, ASA, global)
- How to properly disclose on TikTok (3 methods)
- What counts as "sponsored content" requiring disclosure
- Common disclosure mistakes that lead to violations
- Brand deal contract clauses about disclosures
- International disclosure requirements

---

## Why Disclosure Matters

### Legal Requirements

**In the United States (FTC):**
The Federal Trade Commission requires clear, conspicuous disclosure of any "material connection" between creator and brand.

**Translation:** If you received anything of value (money, free products, affiliate commissions, discounts) in exchange for content, you must disclose it.

**Penalties:**
- First offense: Warning letter
- Repeat offenses: $10,000–$43,280 per violation
- Severe cases: Criminal charges

**In the United Kingdom (ASA):**
The Advertising Standards Authority requires "#ad" or "Ad" label for all paid promotions.

**Penalties:**
- Enforcement action
- Required content removal
- Public reprimand
- Referral to Trading Standards (potential fines)

**In the European Union (EU):**
Local consumer protection laws vary by country but generally align with ASA requirements.

**In Canada (Ad Standards):**
Disclosure required for all sponsored content, following similar principles to FTC.

**In Australia (ACCC):**
Australian Competition and Consumer Commission enforces disclosure for influencer marketing.

### Ethical Reasons

**Beyond legal compliance:**
- Maintains audience trust
- Preserves your credibility and influence
- Protects you legally
- Sets industry standards (creates fair competition)

**Audiences are smart:** They can usually tell when content is sponsored. Hiding it damages trust more than disclosing it.

## What Content Requires Disclosure

### ✅ Always Disclose These

**1. Paid Sponsorships**
- Brand pays you money to create content featuring their product/service
- Flat fee or CPM/CPA payment model
- **Example:** Brand pays $500 for video featuring their product

**2. Free Products or Services**
- Brand sends you free product worth over $50 (approximate threshold)
- Brand provides free service (hotel stay, meal, experience)
- **Example:** Beauty brand sends free $200 palette

**3. Affiliate Links**
- You earn commission if viewers purchase using your link/code
- Even if no guaranteed payment upfront
- **Example:** Amazon Associates link in bio

**4. Brand Partnerships**
- Ongoing relationship with brand (ambassador, sponsored athlete)
- Exclusive partnerships
- **Example:** 6-month brand ambassador contract

**5. Gifted Products You Promote**
- Brand sent unsolicited product, you choose to feature it
- Must disclose even if not required by brand
- **Exception:** Didn't ask for it, don't promote it, just happen to be using in background

**6. Employee/Owner Promoting Own Brand**
- You work for or own the brand you're promoting
- **Example:** You own a supplement company and promote your products

**7. Spark Ads Authorization**
- Brand pays to boost your organic content as an ad
- Disclosure should be in original video (before Spark Ads deal)

### ❌ No Disclosure Needed

**1. Products You Purchased Yourself**
- No relationship with brand
- Genuine personal recommendation
- **Example:** "I love this coffee maker I bought"

**2. General Topic Education**
- Teaching about a category without promoting specific brand
- **Example:** "How to choose running shoes" (no specific brand)

**3. Competitor Comparison (Unsponsored)**
- Comparing products you bought yourself for educational purposes
- **Example:** "iPhone vs Samsung" (neither paid you)

**4. Unsolicited Gift Under $50**
- Brand sent small gift without asking for content
- You mention it casually but don't "promote" it
- **Gray area:** Some creators disclose anyway to be safe

## How to Properly Disclose on TikTok

### Method 1: TikTok's "Branded Content" Toggle (BEST PRACTICE)

**How to use:**
1. Create TikTok video as normal
2. Before posting, tap "Add link"
3. Enable "Brand Content" toggle
4. Add brand name
5. Post video

**What viewers see:**
- "Paid partnership with [Brand]" label at top of video
- Visible on video player
- Cannot be hidden or removed

**Pros:**
- Official TikTok feature (highest compliance)
- Clear and conspicuous (meets FTC requirements)
- Helps TikTok algorithm categorize content correctly
- Required for TikTok Creator Marketplace campaigns

**Cons:**
- None—this is the gold standard

**When to use:** Always, for any paid partnership.

---

### Method 2: #ad or #sponsored in Caption (REQUIRED BACKUP)

**How to use:**
- Include #ad or #sponsored in caption
- Place at the BEGINNING of caption (not buried at end)
- Use full hashtag (not abbreviations)

**Examples:**

**✅ Good:**
> #ad Loving this new skincare routine from @BrandName! Here's why it works…

**❌ Bad:**
> Check out this amazing product! Link in bio! #beauty #skincare #selfcare #love #viral #fyp #partner

(Disclosure buried, too vague)

**Accepted disclosure hashtags:**
- #ad ✅ (most clear)
- #sponsored ✅
- #paidpartnership ✅
- #spon ❌ (not clear enough per FTC)
- #partner ❌ (too ambiguous)
- #collab ❌ (could mean unpaid collaboration)

**FTC requirement:** Disclosure must be "unavoidable"—viewers shouldn't need to click "more" to see it.

**Pros:**
- Simple to implement
- Works if you forget Branded Content toggle
- Reinforces disclosure

**Cons:**
- Can be missed if caption is long
- Not as clear as official toggle

**When to use:** Always, in addition to Branded Content toggle (belt and suspenders approach).

---

### Method 3: Verbal Disclosure in Video (RECOMMENDED)

**How to use:**
Say out loud in the video that content is sponsored.

**Examples:**

**✅ Good:**
> "Thanks to [Brand] for sponsoring today's video!"
> "This video is in partnership with [Brand]."
> "[Brand] sent me this product to try, and here's my honest review."

**❌ Bad:**
> "I'm working with a brand today" (doesn't name brand)
> Showing "AD" text on screen for 0.5 seconds (too quick)

**Pros:**
- Impossible to miss
- Feels natural and authentic
- Builds trust (transparent from the start)

**Cons:**
- None, really

**When to use:** Always, especially if the video might be reposted without caption.

---

### The Triple Disclosure Method (BEST PRACTICE)

**For maximum compliance and transparency:**
1. ✅ Enable TikTok Branded Content toggle
2. ✅ Add #ad at beginning of caption
3. ✅ Say "sponsored by [Brand]" verbally in video

**Why all three?**
- Covers you legally (redundancy)
- Meets all platform and regulatory requirements
- Builds audience trust
- Protects you if TikTok removes one disclosure method

## Common Disclosure Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Disclosure Buried in Caption

**Wrong:**
> Obsessed with my morning routine! Coffee, skincare, journaling... [100 more words] ...#beauty #morning #routine #skincare #selfcare #ad

**Problem:** Disclosure is too far down; viewers may not see it without clicking "more."

**Fix:** Put #ad at the very beginning.

---

### Mistake 2: Using Vague Terms

**Wrong:**
> #collab #partner #sponsored (but without #ad)

**Problem:** FTC says disclosure must be "clear and unambiguous." #partner could mean unpaid collaboration.

**Fix:** Use #ad or #sponsored explicitly.

---

### Mistake 3: Disclosure Only in "Link in Bio"

**Wrong:**
> Check link in bio! (bio says "affiliate link")

**Problem:** Viewers must click through to see disclosure—not "unavoidable."

**Fix:** Disclose in video caption and verbally.

---

### Mistake 4: Assuming Brand Will Disclose for You

**Wrong:**
"The brand will add a disclaimer when they repost my video."

**Problem:** You are legally responsible for disclosure in your original content.

**Fix:** Always disclose, regardless of brand's plans.

---

### Mistake 5: Not Disclosing "Gifted" Products

**Wrong:**
> Brand sent this for free (no payment), so I don't need to disclose.

**Problem:** FTC says free products = material connection requiring disclosure (if over nominal value, ~$50).

**Fix:** Disclose with "#gifted" or "#ad" (safer).

---

### Mistake 6: Removing Disclosure After Posting

**Wrong:**
"I'll post with #ad, then edit caption to remove it after brand approves."

**Problem:** Illegal and unethical. FTC monitors content at time of posting and after.

**Fix:** Disclosure must remain for the life of the content.

---

### Mistake 7: Relying on Spark Ads to Disclose

**Wrong:**
"The brand will run Spark Ads on my video, which will show 'Sponsored'—that's enough."

**Problem:** Original video must have disclosure before brand boosts it.

**Fix:** Disclose in your original organic post.

## Special Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Affiliate Links

**Requirement:**
Disclose affiliate relationships even if you haven't earned a commission yet.

**How:**
> #ad My favorite [product category]! Use code CREATOR10 for 10% off (I earn a small commission)

**Or:**
> #affiliate Links in bio earn me a commission at no extra cost to you.

---

### Scenario 2: Unboxing "Gifted" PR Packages

**Requirement:**
If brand sent you product (even unsolicited), disclose if you're promoting it.

**How:**
> #gifted [Brand] sent me this PR package. Here's my honest review!

**When you don't need to disclose:**
If brand sent unsolicited product and you choose NOT to feature it positively (e.g., neutral mention or negative review without promotion).

---

### Scenario 3: Long-Term Brand Ambassador

**Requirement:**
Disclose in every video, even if audience "knows" you're an ambassador.

**How:**
> As a [Brand] partner, I want to share… #ad

**Don't assume:** Viewers may be new and not know about your relationship.

---

### Scenario 4: Giveaway Sponsored by Brand

**Requirement:**
Disclose that brand is sponsoring the giveaway (even if giveaway is "free" for you).

**How:**
> #ad Giveaway in partnership with [Brand]! Here's how to enter…

**Legal note:** Also follow FTC rules for giveaways (official rules, no purchase necessary, etc.).

---

### Scenario 5: "Seeding" Campaigns (Try Before It's Released)

**Requirement:**
If brand sent you unreleased product to create hype, that's a material connection—disclose.

**How:**
> #ad [Brand] sent me their new product early! Here's my first look…

## International Disclosure Requirements

### United States (FTC)

**Standard:** "Clear and conspicuous" disclosure

**Acceptable methods:**
- #ad, #sponsored (at beginning)
- "Paid partnership" label
- Verbal disclosure

**Reference:** FTC Endorsement Guides (16 CFR Part 255)

---

### United Kingdom (ASA / CAP)

**Standard:** "#Ad" or "Ad" label required

**Acceptable methods:**
- #Ad (exactly this format, capital A)
- "Ad" or "Advert" written out
- TikTok Branded Content toggle

**Not acceptable:** #spon, #collab, #partner (too vague)

**Reference:** CAP Code Section 2 (Recognition of Marketing Communications)

---

### European Union

**Varies by country, but generally:**
- Disclosure must be clear and immediate
- Must be in local language (e.g., #Werbung in Germany, #Publicité in France)
- Most creators use #ad for international audiences

**Check local regulations** if targeting specific EU country.

---

### Canada

**Standard:** Follow Ad Standards guidelines (similar to FTC)

**Acceptable:** #ad, #sponsored, Branded Content label

**Reference:** Canadian Code of Advertising Standards

---

### Australia

**Standard:** Clear disclosure required (ACCC enforcement)

**Acceptable:** #ad, #sponsored, "Paid partnership"

**Reference:** Australian Consumer Law

## What to Include in Brand Contracts About Disclosures

### Standard Contract Clause

**Include this language:**

> **Disclosure Requirements**
> Creator agrees to clearly and conspicuously disclose the paid partnership in accordance with FTC guidelines and TikTok's branded content policies. Creator will use TikTok's Branded Content toggle, include "#ad" at the beginning of the caption, and verbally disclose the partnership in the video.
>
> Creator is solely responsible for ensuring proper disclosure and compliance with applicable laws. Brand is not liable for Creator's failure to disclose.

---

### Red Flag Contract Clauses

**Watch out for brands requesting:**

❌ "Do not use #ad" → Illegal, refuse this

❌ "Disclosure can be subtle" → FTC says it must be clear

❌ "You can remove disclosure after 24 hours" → Illegal

**If brand requests these:** Educate them or walk away. Protect yourself legally.

## Consequences of Not Disclosing

### For Creators

**FTC Enforcement:**
- Warning letter (first offense, usually)
- Fines: $43,280 per violation (max)
- Required to take training on endorsement guidelines
- Public record of violation (affects future brand deals)

**Recent example (2023):** Multiple influencers fined $10K–$50K each for non-disclosure.

**TikTok Enforcement:**
- Content removal
- Account suspension or ban
- Loss of Creator Marketplace access
- Flagged as "unreliable partner" to brands

**Brand Consequences:**
- Damage to reputation
- Loss of future brand deals (word spreads among brands)
- Breach of contract (you may need to refund payment)

**Audience Trust:**
- Loss of credibility
- Unfollows and negative comments
- Harder to convert recommendations

---

### For Brands

Brands are also liable for influencer non-disclosure:
- FTC can fine brands for not requiring creators to disclose
- Public relations damage
- Legal liability

**Result:** Brands increasingly require proof of disclosure and train creators on compliance.

## FAQ

### Do I need to disclose in every video if I'm a long-term ambassador?
Yes. Even if followers "know" you're an ambassador, each video must disclose the relationship. New viewers may not know.

### Can I just say "gifted" instead of "ad"?
Legally yes, but #ad is clearer and safer. "Gifted" can be ambiguous (gift from a friend vs brand).

### What if I genuinely love the product—do I still disclose?
Yes! Disclosure isn't about whether you like the product; it's about whether you received value (payment, free product) from the brand.

### Can the brand edit my caption to remove #ad?
No, and you should not allow this. You are responsible for disclosure remaining in place.

### What if the video is reposted without disclosure?
If you posted with proper disclosure and someone else reposts without it, you're not liable. But always disclose in your original content.

### Do I disclose if I purchased the product with a creator discount?
Yes, if the discount was given specifically because of your creator status (not a public promo).

### What about Spark Ads—does the brand's "Sponsored" label count?
No. Your original video needs disclosure before the brand uses it as a Spark Ad.
